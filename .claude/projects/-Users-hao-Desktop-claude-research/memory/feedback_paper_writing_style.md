Several writing style corrections from the ai-energy-ot paper session:

1. **No bullet-point findings in Introduction.** Present results as flowing prose paragraphs, not numbered lists. Save detailed numbers for the Results section; intro should preview headline results in 2-3 sentences.

2. **Minimal em-dashes (---).** Use sparingly — only where they do real structural work (e.g., defining an appositive clause). Replace most with commas, colons, parentheses, or sentence breaks. Target ~1 per 4 pages.

3. **No "The remainder of the paper is organised as follows" boilerplate.** Standard section structure doesn't need signposting. If the paper has a non-standard structure, describe it naturally.

4. **No "to our knowledge, this is the first..."** Sounds unprofessional. Instead, cite the relevant review paper and state the gap factually (e.g., "A review of 28 studies finds none use X").

5. **No stylistic italics (\emph) for emphasis.** Keep italics only for constraint/item labels in enumerations. Don't italicise terms like "where", "when", "spatial", "temporal", "geometry", etc.

6. **Subsections/paragraphs should flow with transitions, not fragment with headers.** The lit review and results sections should read as continuous narrative. Use transition sentences between topics instead of \subsection or \paragraph headers (except in Methodology where cross-references require labels).

7. **Don't repeat the abstract in the Introduction.** The intro findings paragraph should add depth/implications, not restate the same numbers in the same order.

8. **Every assumption needs evidence or honest framing.** If numbers are assumed (e.g., demand shares), say "assumed shares" not "proportional to population density" without a source. Add a sentence on why the assumption doesn't affect conclusions.

**Why:** Hao values clean, professional academic prose that flows naturally. The paper should read like a journal article, not a technical report.

**How to apply:** When drafting any paper section, write flowing paragraphs first. Add headers only where cross-references require them. Check for em-dash overuse, italic overuse, and boilerplate before presenting.
